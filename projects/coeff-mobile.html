CoEFFICIENT Mobile

Native iOS applications for field teams, optimized for weak network connections and offline functionality.

The Problem

Field teams work in environments with spotty connectivity—parking lots, service bays, remote locations. The web-based CoEFFICIENT platform required constant connectivity and wasn't optimized for mobile. Teams were missing critical information because they couldn't access the system in the field, or data was stale by the time they got online.

The Goal

Build native iOS applications that brought the full power of CoEFFICIENT into field teams' pockets. The app needed to work offline, sync intelligently when connectivity returned, and provide the most critical information in a mobile-first interface optimized for quick decisions in the field.

Design Approach

We designed mobile experiences that prioritized the most important actions and information for field teams. Every screen was optimized for one-handed use in real-world conditions. We built a sophisticated offline-first architecture that allowed teams to work anywhere, anytime, and sync seamlessly when they regained connectivity.

Key Features

  • Offline-first architecture with smart syncing
  • Role-based mobile experiences
  • One-handed navigation and interaction
  • Essential metrics at a glance
  • Real-time notifications and alerts
  • Fingerprint and Face ID authentication

Results & Impact

  • 100% of field teams adopted the mobile app within 6 months
  • Field team engagement increased 3x compared to web platform
  • Reduced time to access critical information from minutes to seconds
  • Eliminated connectivity as a barrier to field execution
  • Set new standard for mobile-first enterprise apps

Learnings

This project taught me that truly understanding the context where your users work is critical. By spending time in the field and seeing how teams actually worked (often in conditions very different from a clean office), we designed an app that felt natural in their environment. Mobile isn't just a smaller web experience—it's a fundamentally different interaction paradigm that requires different thinking.
