ROIKOI Referral Platform

Talent mapping software leveraging employee networks to hire people others want to work with.

The Problem

Traditional recruiting was broken. Job boards attracted a wide net but often the wrong candidates. The best hiring happened through referrals—employees knew who they wanted to work with. But referral processes were clunky, incentives were unclear, and great candidates in employees' networks were never discovered. There had to be a better way to tap into the networks that already existed inside every organization.

The Goal

Create a platform that made referrals frictionless and rewarding. Instead of fighting network effects, we wanted to weaponize them. Employees should be able to easily recommend their network, and organizations should be able to discover candidates through the lens of "people your employees want to work with"—not just "people who filled out an application."

Design Approach

We built a platform that made referrals social and rewarding. Employees could easily browse their LinkedIn network, refer people with one click, and track referrals through to hire. The company gained visibility into its network and could identify talent clusters and communities. We gamified the experience without making it feel silly—real incentives for real behavior change.

Key Features

  • One-click LinkedIn integration and referrals
  • Social recognition and leaderboards
  • Network visualization and talent mapping
  • Referral tracking and attribution
  • Incentive management and rewards
  • Analytics on referral sources and quality

Results & Impact

  • Increased referral pipeline by 400% within first year
  • Reduced time-to-hire for referred candidates by 50%
  • Improved quality of referred candidates (higher retention, performance)
  • Created competition among teams to have the strongest networks
  • Platform acquired by Terminal in 2020, validating the network model

Learnings

Starting my own company taught me that the best products solve real problems in fundamentally new ways. Referral hiring wasn't new—but making it effortless and rewarding was. By removing friction and tapping into existing social dynamics, we created something that felt inevitable in hindsight. The acquisition validated that we had found a real problem worth solving at scale.
